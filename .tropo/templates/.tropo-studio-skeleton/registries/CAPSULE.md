# `.tropo-studio/registries/` — Identity & Index Surfaces

## Purpose

Machine-readable registries that the vault's agents + tooling query to answer:

- **Who are the agents in this vault?** → `agent-registry.yaml` (hand-maintained class + identity records)
- **What sa.\*/skills/tools are callable?** → `registry.jsonl` (typed runtime catalog, regenerated by `scripts/rebuild-registry.ts`)
- **Where are work artifacts indexed?** → `vault/00-index.jsonl` (regenerated by `.tropo/scripts/rebuild-vault.py`) — outside this folder per matched-primitives topology

Each domain owns its discovery primitive. There is no single universal UID registry — see [Registry Topology Consolidation](../../../../vault/files/adac1f10.md) for the architectural framing.

## What belongs here

- **`agent-registry.yaml`** — roster of registered agents on this vault. Hand-maintained class + identity records (governance-as-data). Schema declared in the file's header. Ships with appropriate first-boot scaffolding.
- **`registry.jsonl`** — runtime callable catalog (sa.\*/skills/tools) projected from source frontmatter via the rebuilder. Ships empty; regenerated as content lands.
- Additional registries as your vault's patterns evolve.

## What does NOT belong here

- **Ledger files** — those live in `vault/files/<uid>.md` if your vault uses the ledger pattern.
- **Agent activation files** — those live in `agents/<name>/`.
- **Historical run records** — those live in `runs/` or the engineering log, not in registries.

## Who writes here

- **Concierge** — populates during first-vault-setup.
- **Vault-admin delegate** — if you designate an operations agent, they maintain registries.
- **Grooming agents** — scheduled fleet members may update indexes at their scheduled cadence.

Not a free-write zone. Registry writes are deliberate; the boot playbook and commission protocols coordinate updates.

## On first boot

`agent-registry.yaml` ships with `files: {}` — this is the CORRECT first-run state, not a bug. The first-vault-setup playbook will register your first agent, and every subsequent governed-agent creation adds a record.
