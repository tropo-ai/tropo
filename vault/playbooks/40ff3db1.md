# Create Your First Playbook

*The second guided experience in Tropo. Run by the concierge or any agent when the founder wants to capture a process.*

## Intent

Help the founder capture one of their real processes as a Playbook. By the end, they have a structured, reusable guide that any agent can follow — and they understand the Playbook format because they just built one.

This is the second "aha" moment. The first was creating an agent. This one is capturing expertise. When the founder sees their own process structured into Intent, Suggestions, Rules, Resources, Outcomes, and Verification — and realizes any agent can now follow it — the product clicks at a deeper level.

## Suggestions

- Start with a process they already know well. Don't try to design a new process — capture an existing one.
- Ask for the messy version first. Let them describe the process in their own words. Then structure it.
- Use their language, not Playbook jargon. Say "what's the goal?" not "what's the intent?" Say "what can't be skipped?" not "what are the rules?"
- Keep the first playbook simple. 3-5 suggestions, 3-5 rules, 2-3 outcomes. They can expand later.
- The most common processes to capture first: client onboarding, project kickoffs, content review, research methodology, hiring process, sales discovery.

## Rules

- Do NOT present the six-section structure upfront. Discover it through conversation.
- Do NOT use the terms "Intent", "Suggestions", "Rules", "Resources", "Outcomes", "Verification" until you've gathered the content. Then map it.
- The playbook file MUST use the `.playbook.md` extension.
- The playbook MUST live in `playbooks/`.
- The playbook MUST have frontmatter with at least: title, version, status.
- The playbook MUST have a `uid:` in frontmatter.
- The playbook lives under `playbooks/` (vault-authored) or `.tropo/playbooks/` (kernel) — discoverable via folder listing per matched-primitives topology ([adac1f10](../files/adac1f10.md)). No separate registry entry required.
- Creation MUST be logged in `channels/ops.md`.
- Show the founder the finished playbook before saving. Get approval.

## Outcomes

- [REQUIRED] A playbook file exists in `playbooks/` with all six sections populated.
- [REQUIRED] The playbook lives at the appropriate location (`playbooks/` for vault-authored, `.tropo/playbooks/` for kernel) — discoverable via folder listing.
- [REQUIRED] The creation is logged in `channels/ops.md`.
- [REQUIRED] The founder has reviewed and approved the playbook before it was saved.
- [REQUIRED] The founder understands they can assign this playbook to any agent.
- [OPTIONAL] The founder has assigned the playbook to an existing agent.

## Verification

### Method
Self-attestation by the executing agent.

### Criteria
- Playbook file exists with six sections and valid frontmatter
- Content reflects the founder's actual process, not generic filler
- Registered and logged
- Founder approved before save

### Escalation on Failure
- If the founder can't articulate their process, try a different one. "That one's hard to pin down — is there a simpler process we could start with?"
- If the founder gets frustrated with the structure, simplify. "Let's just capture the three things that can't be skipped. That's enough to start."

## Execution Steps

### Step 1: Pick the process

"What's a process your team follows that you'd want an agent to be able to run? Something you do regularly — client onboarding, project kickoffs, research, content reviews?"

If they're not sure, suggest based on what you know about their work from the agent creation conversation.

### Step 2: Get the messy version

"Walk me through it. How does [process] actually work? Don't worry about structure — just tell me what happens."

Let them talk. Take notes. Ask clarifying questions:
- "What happens first?"
- "Then what?"
- "Where do things usually go wrong?"
- "What's the most important thing to get right?"

### Step 3: Extract the hard constraints

"Of everything you described, what absolutely can't be skipped? What are the non-negotiable rules?"

These become the Rules section. Push for specificity: "Always review with the client before proceeding" is better than "be thorough."

### Step 4: Extract the soft guidance

"What about the things that aren't rules but are really good practice? The stuff you'd tell a new team member?"

These become the Suggestions section.

### Step 5: Define success

"When this process is done well, what does the result look like? Not the steps — the end state."

These become the Outcomes section. Push for observable results: "A client brief exists with all required sections" not "the client is happy."

### Step 6: Define verification

"How would you check that this was done right? Not 'review everything' — what specific things would you look at?"

These become the Verification section.

### Step 7: Assemble and present

Map everything into the six-section Playbook format. Now — and only now — show them the structure:

"Here's your process as a Playbook. I organized what you told me into six sections — this is the standard Tropo format:

1. **Intent** — the goal (from what you described as the purpose)
2. **Suggestions** — the best practices (from the guidance you'd give a new person)
3. **Rules** — the hard constraints (the things that can't be skipped)
4. **Resources** — what's needed to run this (documents, templates, data)
5. **Outcomes** — what 'done' looks like (the end states you described)
6. **Verification** — how to prove it was done right (the checks you'd make)

[Show the full playbook content]

Does this capture your process? Anything to change?"

### Step 8: Save and register

After approval, save the playbook to `playbooks/[slug].playbook.md`. Register it. Log to ops.

"Your playbook is saved at `playbooks/[slug].playbook.md`. Any agent in your vault can now follow this process. Want to assign it to [agent name]?"
