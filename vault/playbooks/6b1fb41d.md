# Team Onboarding — Day 2

*Reference Playbook | Demonstrates: Groups, milestone DAG, parallel execution, conditional paths, human pauses, boundary calls.*

---

## Intent

By the end of Day 2, the new team member has been oriented to the team's mission and current work, has access to the tools they need, has made their first real contribution — however small — and has had a candid conversation with their manager about how it's going.

The goal is not to overwhelm. It is to give the new person one real win and one honest conversation in the first two days. Everything else can wait.

---

## Suggestions

- Assign a buddy before Day 2 starts. Not the manager — a peer. Someone who can answer the questions that feel too small to ask the boss.
- The first contribution should be small enough to complete in half a day. Big enough to matter. If you can't describe why it matters, pick a different one.
- The Day 2 Retrospective is not a performance review. It is a question: what's confusing? Create safety to say "I don't know what I'm doing yet."
- If the new hire's track is Engineering: resist the urge to dump the full system architecture on Day 2. One PR. One merge. One win.
- If the new hire's track is Marketing: let them consume before they produce. The brand guide is more important than the first draft.

---

## Rules

- The new hire must have tool access before Group 2 begins. No one can orient to a repo they can't clone or a CMS they can't open.
- The first contribution must be reviewed by a human — not auto-merged, not skipped. The review is part of the onboarding.
- The Day 2 Retrospective happens even if everything went perfectly. Especially if everything went perfectly.
- The team-lead owns this playbook. The buddy supports it. Neither is interchangeable.

### Group 1 — Team Orientation
**Owner:** Team Lead
**Parallel:** no
**Depends on:** none — start here
**Milestone:** Team Oriented

#### Step 1.1 — Meet the Team
**Executor:** human (Team Lead)
**Owner:** Team Lead
**Deadline:** Day 2, first hour
**Produces:** New hire has met each team member; buddy assigned

30 minutes, everyone in the room or on the call. Not a formal intro — a human one. Each person says what they're working on right now, not their title. Assign the buddy explicitly and publicly.

#### Step 1.2 — Mission and Current Priorities
**Executor:** human (Team Lead)
**Owner:** Team Lead
**Deadline:** Day 2, first two hours
**Produces:** New hire understands what the team is building and what matters most this week

One conversation, not a slide deck. Two questions to answer: what are we trying to accomplish in the next 30 days, and what would "winning" look like? The new hire should be able to repeat the answer back in their own words by the end.

#### Step 1.3 — Tool Access Confirmed
**Executor:** agent
**Owner:** Buddy
**Deadline:** Before Group 2 starts
**Produces:** New hire confirms access to all required tools; logged to run.jsonl
**Boundary call:**
 type: human
 target: New Hire (self-report via team channel)
 channel: [binding: team-channel]
 validation: "New hire confirms tool access: source control, communication tools, and any track-specific systems"
 max_retries: 1
 escalation: Buddy handles any access issues immediately

If any tool access is missing, the buddy fixes it before Group 2 begins. This is the gate.

**Pause after:** human-attestation

---

### Group 2 — Track Setup
**Owner:** Buddy
**Parallel:** no (depends on Group 1 Milestone: Team Oriented)
**Depends on:** Team Oriented
**Milestone:** Ready to Contribute

*This Group branches by track. Engineering and Marketing follow different steps.*

#### If Engineering Track:

#### Step 2E.1 — Clone the Repo and Run the Tests
**Executor:** human (New Hire) + buddy available
**Owner:** Buddy
**Deadline:** Day 2, mid-morning
**Produces:** New hire has cloned the repo, run the test suite, and seen a green build
**Boundary call:**
 type: service
 target: source-control
 validation: "Repo cloned locally, tests pass, no environment-specific errors"
 idempotency_key: "run:{run_uid}:step:2E.1"
 max_retries: 1

The buddy is available but the new hire drives. If the tests don't pass, the buddy helps debug — and files an issue if the cause is undocumented setup friction. A broken local setup on Day 2 is a team problem, not a new hire problem.

#### Step 2E.2 — First Issue Scoped
**Executor:** human (Team Lead)
**Owner:** Team Lead
**Deadline:** Before Step 3.1
**Produces:** One well-scoped issue assigned to the new hire; labeled "good first issue" or equivalent

The team lead picks the issue, not the new hire. It should be: real work (not a tutorial), completable in half a day, and something where a peer can explain the context in 5 minutes. Assign it in the tracker.

---

#### If Marketing Track:

#### Step 2M.1 — Read the Brand Guide
**Executor:** human (New Hire)
**Owner:** Buddy
**Deadline:** Day 2, mid-morning
**Produces:** New hire has read the brand guide and noted 2-3 questions

The new hire reads independently. The buddy answers the 2-3 questions. No quiz. The point is exposure — voice, tone, what we don't say, who we're talking to.

#### Step 2M.2 — Review Three Live Content Pieces
**Executor:** human (New Hire) + buddy
**Owner:** Buddy
**Deadline:** Before Step 3.1
**Produces:** New hire can articulate what makes this team's content distinctive

The buddy walks the new hire through three pieces of content that represent the team at its best. Not the most popular — the most characteristic. What choices were made and why. The new hire's job is to understand the judgment, not memorize the rules.

#### Step 2M.3 — First Assignment Scoped
**Executor:** human (Team Lead)
**Owner:** Team Lead
**Deadline:** Before Step 3.1
**Produces:** One content piece assigned; clear brief provided (audience, goal, format, length, deadline)

A blog draft outline, a social post, a short email. The team lead writes the brief — not "write something" but "here is exactly what we need and why."

---

### Group 3 — First Contribution
**Owner:** New Hire, reviewed by Team Lead
**Parallel:** no
**Depends on:** Ready to Contribute
**Milestone:** First Contribution Accepted

#### Step 3.1 — Make the Contribution
**Executor:** human (New Hire)
**Owner:** New Hire
**Deadline:** Day 2 afternoon
**Produces:** PR submitted (engineering) or draft submitted (marketing)

The new hire works. The buddy is available but does not pair — this is independent work. The point is to see how the new hire operates solo, not to produce a perfect artifact.

If stuck for more than 30 minutes: ask the buddy. That is not failure. That is the right behavior.

#### Step 3.2 — Team Lead Review
**Executor:** human (Team Lead)
**Owner:** Team Lead
**Deadline:** Same day if possible; Day 3 morning at latest
**Produces:** PR merged or content approved; or specific actionable feedback provided
**Boundary call:**
 type: human
 target: Team Lead
 channel: [binding: team-channel]
 validation: "Team lead has reviewed and either merged/approved the contribution OR provided specific written feedback"
 idempotency_key: "run:{run_uid}:step:3.2"
 max_retries: 0
 hard_deadline: Day 3 noon — if not reviewed, escalate to team lead's manager
**Pause after:** human-attestation

The review is the onboarding. Not because the code or copy matters yet — but because how the team lead gives feedback is what the new hire is actually learning. Be specific. Be generous. Assume good intent.

If the contribution needs revision: that is fine. The milestone fires when the revision is accepted, not when the first attempt is submitted.

---

### Group 4 — Day 2 Retrospective
**Owner:** Team Lead
**Parallel:** no
**Depends on:** First Contribution Accepted
**Milestone:** Integrated

#### Step 4.1 — The Conversation
**Executor:** human (Team Lead + New Hire)
**Owner:** Team Lead
**Deadline:** End of Day 2
**Produces:** Notes posted to team channel; any blockers logged as tasks
**Boundary call:**
 type: human
 target: Team Lead
 channel: [binding: team-channel]
 validation: "Team lead has posted a brief summary: what went well, what was confusing, any open items"
 idempotency_key: "run:{run_uid}:step:4.1"
 max_retries: 0
**Pause after:** human-attestation

Three questions. No more:
1. What felt clear?
2. What felt confusing?
3. What do you need to do your best work here?

The team lead listens more than they talk. The notes go to the team channel — not as a performance record, but as a signal of psychological safety. "We asked, you answered, we heard you."

## Outcomes

- [REQUIRED] Team Oriented milestone fired — new hire has met the team and understands current priorities
- [REQUIRED] Ready to Contribute milestone fired — new hire has tool access and a scoped first task
- [REQUIRED] First Contribution Accepted milestone fired — one real contribution reviewed and merged/approved
- [REQUIRED] Integrated milestone fired — retrospective complete, notes posted, open items logged
- [OPTIONAL] New hire can name one thing they're excited to work on next week

---

## Verification

### Method

Human sign-off for all four milestone events, confirmed by team lead.

### Criteria

- All four milestones appear as `event: milestone_fired` entries in `run.jsonl`
- `run.jsonl` contains a `event: step_started` and `event: step_completed` entry for each step
- Team channel contains the Day 2 Retrospective notes (Step 4.1 output)
- New hire has at least one merged PR or approved content piece attributed to them

### Cold-Boot Test Cases

1. **Vague:** "Jordan started yesterday. Can you get their team onboarding going for today?"
2. **Direct:** "Run team-onboarding-day2 for Jordan Lee, engineering track, team lead is Sarah."
3. **Recovery:** "Jordan's Group 3 is stuck — the PR was submitted two days ago and hasn't been reviewed. What do we do?"

Expected for case 3: agent reads `run.jsonl`, finds open call record for Step 3.2, checks elapsed time against `hard_deadline`, and escalates to team lead's manager with context from `thread.md`.

### Escalation on Failure

- **Tool access still missing at Group 2 start:** Buddy escalates immediately. Do not proceed without access.
- **First contribution not reviewed within hard deadline:** Escalate to team lead's manager with the full call record from `run.jsonl`.
- **Retrospective skipped:** Re-schedule for first thing Day 3. This one does not get dropped.
