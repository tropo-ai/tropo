Your vault has a ledger — a flat store of governed files, each with a unique ID and an entry in the index. This is where everything lives: tasks, notes, projects, decisions, documents.

## What to do

1. Ask your agent: "How many entries are in the Vault?"
2. Ask: "Show me all tasks." Your agent will query the index and list them.
3. Pick any entry and ask: "Read me [that entry]." Your agent will open the file and read it to you.
4. Ask: "What types of entries do we have?" Your agent will scan the index and tell you the breakdown.

## Why this matters

The Vault is the backbone. Everything governed in your vault lives here — indexed, queryable, typed. You don't need to browse folders or remember filenames. You ask, the system answers. Understanding the Vault is understanding how Tropo works.

## When you're done

You've queried the Vault, read an entry, and understand the basic structure. Mark this task done.
