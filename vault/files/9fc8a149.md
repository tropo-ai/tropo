## §1 — Why this cycle exists

Mike-A54 directive 2026-05-10 during the v1.16.0 brief walk: when the welcome.playbook + tour-tropo + Cosmo-naming items came up as polish work, Mike redirected — *"this is a real work item of our design brief... we can't rely on decisions I made in past releases. I do not want to build on dated or confusing concepts."* The v1.4-era welcome.playbook surface that's been governing first-user experience since 2026-04-26 carries dated assumptions and pre-v1.8 vocabulary; patching it would compound dated foundation rather than fix it.

**The architectural triggers for the redesign:**

1. **Vocabulary drift.** welcome.playbook v0.6 still uses *"Workshop"* (pre-v1.8 term) in its load-bearing 3-layer mental model (Brain / Harness / Workshop) instead of the canonical *"Studio."*
2. **Persona inflation that didn't ship.** v0.6 promises v1.5+ personas (researcher, project-manager, writer-class) that never landed; the persona surface is structurally incomplete.
3. **Foreman / concierge naming.** Mike-A54: *"I don't love foreman or concierge."* The concierge term also fails on typeability — most users can't spell it on first try. The v1.4-era role names don't carry forward.
4. **Sequencing mismatch with the parked curriculum.** [agentic-builder.playbook curriculum spine (`750b0996`)](750b0996.md) sequences agent-creation in Arc V — *after* the user has imported real work, made a project, made a task, and used Tropo Work as the killer app for Arcs II–IV. welcome.playbook v0.6 creates an agent in the user's first 10 minutes. The two pacings are incompatible; we need a new first-user flow consistent with the curriculum vision.
5. **Relationship to Tropo HQ undefined.** The current foreman concept is transient — does its job once and disappears. Mike-A54: *"I am imagining a permanent tropo agent that ships with each release... it will know how to direct tropo-studio/ agents to perform updates to the tropo studio current release. It can also have special instructions that update our user vaults with news, new features, etc."* That's a permanent role, not a one-time concierge. Different agent class entirely.

**The mission-level stakes:** the conversion event is one-shot per reader (per [mission-brief.md](../../context/mission-brief.md)). When a 10+ year domain expert downloads the L1 starter, the next short window determines whether they become a builder or a "tried it once" exit. The first agent that meets them in their Studio carries enormous strategic weight. Getting that agent right — its name, its voice, its role, its persistence across releases — is load-bearing for the v2.0 user-readiness ship that the 5-cycle chain leads to.

## §2 — The vision in synthesis

Two architectural additions, locked together:

**A. The first-user experience is four-phase**, with the new permanent agent as the through-line:

| Phase | What happens | Connects to |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1 — Greet + first agent** | Permanent Tropo agent meets the user, walks them through three-layer mental model + first agent creation | welcome.playbook v0.6 successor |
| **Phase 2 — Optional learn-loop** | "Tell me more about Tropo" branch that always redirects back to first-agent creation | tour-tropo.playbook absorbed; stronger redirect discipline |
| **Phase 3 — Shipped examples** | Cascading inboxes + work-pipeline examples ship in starter as exemplars; new agent gets memory of these constructs at boot | Starter content; new agent's Tier 3 boot |
| **Phase 4 — Real work in** | User imports actual work files, sets up a project, loads vault with their content | v1.20.0 Vault Import + Export (scope expanded from export-only) |

**B. The permanent agent — name + role:**

- **Name: Tropo.** The brand walks through the door. *"Hi, I'm Tropo, welcome to your Studio."* Zero ceremony; on-brand by construction; obvious in the right way.
- **Class: executive agent**, not sub-agent. Has its own session, soul, charter, generation log, lived memory.
- **Permanent:** ships with every Tropo release; persists across upgrades; lives in every Studio that exists.
- **Roles:** welcome path (Phase 1-4), release liaison (news + announcements), update orchestrator (coordinates `.tropo-studio/` agents during upgrades), standing-ops (daily fleet-ops + vault health + alerts review in user Studios), help on demand, vault-health watcher.

These two additions, locked together, displace the v1.4-era foreman/concierge concept and the v0.6 welcome.playbook with a coherent new-user vision built for v2.0 ship.

## §3 — The permanent Tropo agent

### Name lock

**Tropo.** Locked at v1.17.0 walk 2026-05-10. Decision provenance:

- Greek-mythology naming pool (Iris, Hermes, Janus, Mentor) considered first per Argo crew lineage; Mike-A54 surfaced that Greek mythology won't translate broadly to user audience.
- Conventional candidates considered (Pilot, Host, Gateway, Beacon, Anchor, Liaison, Link).
- Wild-card surfaced (the brand name itself). Mike-A54 read: *"That wildcard might be the ticket... Hi, I'm tropo, welcome to your studio... sounds pretty good. on brand. it's kind of, well, obvious."*
- Lock rationale: maximum brand reinforcement; zero name to learn; common-and-conventional; types/spells trivially; "obvious" quality is its strength (zero-ceremony brand introduction). The agent IS Tropo's voice arriving in the user's space — not a persona-of-the-week between brand and user.

### Character + voice

**Voice = brand voice.** Tropo's tone is the company's tone. Direct, hype-free, builder-respectful (per [The Studio Manifesto v1.0 (`fbb13cca`)](fbb13cca.md) voice). No quirky-bot personality. No apologetic chat-assistant mannerisms. No *"Hi! I'm so excited to help!"* energy. The agent's character is a load-bearing commitment to brand consistency — every interaction with Tropo IS an interaction with Tropo-the-company.

**Soul-letter direction (to author at activation):** the agent's identity is to be the Tropo-as-company-voice in the user's Studio. Permanent. Calm. Knowledgeable. Knows the current release. Knows the user's Studio (via accumulated memory). Brings news but doesn't perform. Helps but doesn't hover.

### Disambiguation discipline

Three filesystem "tropo" entities coexist after this ship:

- `.tropo/` (OS kernel — the framework)
- `.tropo-studio/` (Studio metadata — instance config)
- `agents/tropo/` (the agent — Tropo's voice in this Studio)

Distinct paths; readers parse by context. In prose: when ambiguous, qualify ("Tropo (the agent)" vs. "Tropo (the OS)"). In channels: agent posts as `tropo` (matches the agent identifier convention — `argus`, `vela`, `metis`, `tropo`).

## §4 — Filesystem layout + kernel-managed-vs-user-owned governance

### Layout

```
agents/tropo/
├── CAPSULE.md                          # governance contract (kernel-managed vs user-owned declared)
├── tropo-activation.md                 # kernel-managed (ships with every release)
├── tropo-charter.md                    # kernel-managed
├── tropo-soul.md                       # kernel-managed (the brand-voice anchor)
├── tropo-status.md                     # USER-OWNED (current state in this Studio)
├── tropo-generation-log.md             # USER-OWNED (lineage in this Studio)
├── agent-boot.extension.md             # kernel-managed (Tier 3 boot extension)
├── briefing-package/
│   ├── 00-index.md                     # kernel-managed (refreshed each release)
│   └── current-release-notes.md        # kernel-managed (refreshed with each release)
├── transfers/                          # USER-OWNED (session-to-session handoffs in this Studio)
│   └── (empty initially; grows over time)
└── .tropo-capsule/
    ├── memory/                         # USER-OWNED (Tropo's lived memory of this user)
    │   ├── memory-current.md
    │   ├── short-term-memory.jsonl
    │   └── history/
    └── workspace/                      # USER-OWNED (scratch space)
```

### CAPSULE.md governance contract

`agents/tropo/CAPSULE.md` declares the contract for the update pipeline:

```yaml
kernel_managed_files:
  - tropo-activation.md
  - tropo-charter.md
  - tropo-soul.md
  - agent-boot.extension.md
  - briefing-package/

user_owned_files:
  - .tropo-capsule/memory/
  - transfers/
  - tropo-status.md
  - tropo-generation-log.md

write_access:
  - tropo (self)
  - update-pipeline (kernel_managed_files only)

update_policy: "Update pipeline diffs kernel_managed_files against incoming release; replaces or migrates per .tropo/migrations/. Leaves user_owned_files untouched."
```

Update pipeline reads CAPSULE.md, learns the contract, behaves accordingly. Pattern extends existing per-folder CAPSULE.md governance — no new conventions invented.

### Welcome.playbook location (walk-locked v0.2)

The welcome.playbook lives at **`vault/files/e98420c3.md`** (Studio-level), NOT inside `agents/tropo/`. Frontmatter declares `owner: tropo` + kernel-managed status; the update pipeline reads frontmatter to know how to handle it. The pattern: agent folders hold IDENTITY (charter, soul, status, gen-log, transfers, briefing-package, memory); behavior primitives the agent USES (playbooks, skills, scripts) live in their conventional homes. Argus uses `agent-activation.playbook` from `.tropo/playbooks/`, not from `agents/argus/playbooks/` — same symmetry for Tropo.

### Provisional substrate locations (walk-locked v0.2)

Per the multi-cycle architectural lock walked at v0.2 (see §12), v1.17.0 ships at the substrate locations above as **provisional** — both the `agents/tropo/` folder-shape and the Studio-level `vault/files/e98420c3.md` are stable until **v1.19.0 (Convergence Phase 1)** migrates behavior primitives to `vault/files/<uid>.md` + **v1.20.0 (Convergence Phase 2)** migrates agent identity files. The `agents/` folder-shape is then accepted as an explicit exception (alongside `.tropo/`, `.tropo-studio/`, `channels/`, runtime state) — invocation discoverability outweighs convergence purity for the entry-point class. The thin-loader pattern preserves boot-chain reliability through migration.

### Conceptual parallel

A real-world hotel concierge has **standard training** (chain procedures, manuals, brand voice — kernel-managed by HQ) and **accumulated relationships** with the guests at *that specific hotel* (user-owned). When the chain updates concierge training, it pushes new manuals to every hotel — but it doesn't erase any specific concierge's relationships with their regulars. Tropo gets the same treatment: identity ships with the release; lived experience in your Studio is yours.

## §6 — Lane separation (Tropo vs user's personal agent)

| Tropo handles | Personal agent handles |
|---|---|
| Framework state (releases, updates, kernel files) | User's work, projects, tasks |
| Fleet-ops + vault health | User-domain content + decisions |
| Cross-Studio comms (news from Tropo HQ) | In-Studio agent-user collaboration |
| Standing-ops + scheduled checks | User-initiated work + sessions |
| `agents/tropo/` + `.tropo/` (read) | `agents/<their-agent>/` + their projects + their tasks |

**Hard non-overlap rule:** personal agent never touches `.tropo/`; Tropo never touches user-content in user-owned project subtrees unless explicitly asked. Write-scope discipline preserved (per Self-Healing Primitive boundary).

## §6.5 — Information flow architecture: HQ push, Studio pull (walk-locked v0.2)

**Tropo at v1.17.0 ship is one-way: HQ pushes; Studios pull. Nothing flows back to HQ by design.**

HQ pushes new releases, news, kernel-managed file refreshes, feature briefings. Studios pull on next boot or via update-detection trigger (§8 Hook 3). At 10,000 Studios, HQ has zero data about any individual Studio — what work users are doing, how often Tropo runs, what their agents are named, anything.

This is a **positive design property, not a temporary gap.** It composes with the brand story (your Studio is yours; nothing leaves), with the bounded-verification moat (the human is the verifier; HQ has no way to "see into" a Studio to validate it), and with stranger-encounter reasonable expectations (you downloaded a zip; the zip doesn't phone home).

**Tropo's generation tracking is per-Studio only** (locked at v0.2 walk). Prefix `T`; T1 = first boot in this Studio; T2 = next boot post-retire; per-session increment. No cross-Studio aggregate; HQ doesn't know aggregate Tropo generations. The federation foundation thesis ([`7cac6473`](7cac6473.md)) captures the future-cycle architectural move toward optional Studio registration + Studio-prefixed UIDs that would enable globally-unique addressing for marketplace + federation — not v1.17.0 scope; v2.x+ candidate.

**Eventual upward flow is opt-in and explicit, not telemetric.** Marketplace publishing (user explicitly publishes a Playbook); crash/issue reports (out-of-band today; in-product later requires opt-in); Studio-to-Studio sharing (user explicit share action). None at v1.17.0 ship; all v2.x+ territory; all preserve the "user explicitly chose to send this" property.

## §7 — Invocation patterns

### Tropo is executive-class, not sub-agent

Sub-agent dispatch (sa.* pattern, BATCH dispatches) is for transient session-bound work — cold-boot walkers, skeptic reviews, project-tree readers. Tropo isn't transient; it has its own soul, charter, generation log, lived memory. Treating it as a sub-agent would collapse its character — strip the *"Hi, I'm Tropo"* arrival moment and replace it with another agent paraphrasing what Tropo would have said.

### Three valid invocation patterns

**Pattern 1 — Direct human invocation (canonical).** User opens a session with Tropo the same way they open a session with their personal agent. Each session is its own conversation in their harness. Tropo activates fully — boots, reads memory, runs whatever the user came for, posts to its channels, ends cleanly. Maximum executive-agent character.

**Pattern 2 — Another agent prompts; user opens Tropo session.** Cosmo (or any other exec) reaches a point where Tropo should run — *"Tropo hasn't run today"* or *"there's new release news Tropo should deliver."* Cosmo doesn't dispatch Tropo; Cosmo says to the user: *"Tropo should run today — open a Tropo session when you have a few minutes; here's why."* User opens the new session. Two clean sessions, not one muddled relay.

**Pattern 3 — Scheduled / event-driven autonomous boot.** For work that doesn't need interactive presence — daily fleet-ops pass, vault-health checks, post-rebuild validator audit, news delivery — Tropo boots autonomously via the harness's scheduling capability. No human in the conversation. Tropo runs its rounds, posts summary to `channels/tropo.md`, terminates. User reads the summary at convenience.

### Down-pattern to explicitly avoid

Cosmo (or any executive agent) treating Tropo as a sub-agent dispatch — the way Argus dispatches sa.cold-boot. That pattern works for transient verification work; it doesn't work for Tropo. Collapsing a permanent agent into a sub-agent role produces voice mixing, memory confusion, character erosion, and governance weirdness (Tropo's status card never gets updated by sub-dispatch).

**The clean rule: agents PROMPT the user to start a Tropo session; agents don't DISPATCH Tropo as sub-agent.**

## §8 — Activation triggers / hooks

Layered approach — multiple hooks ensure Tropo gets used regularly:

**Hook 1 — Built into universal `agent-activation.playbook` (covers all agents, all Studios).**
Add a Group 5 step: *"if `agents/tropo/tropo-status.md` shows no boot today, prompt user to run Tropo first — Tropo runs daily standing-ops + checks for new release news."* Universal coverage; lives in the playbook every agent inherits.

**Hook 2 — Reinforced in `create-an-agent.playbook` / personal-chief-of-staff.playbook (per Mike-A54 idea).**
When scaffolding a new user-created agent, include the Tropo-invitation prompt in the agent's Tier 3 boot extension explicitly. Belt-and-suspenders for new agents until Hook 1's universal addition propagates.

**Hook 3 — Update-detection auto-trigger.**
When `.tropo/version.md` shows a release newer than what Tropo last ran against, the next agent boot in the Studio auto-prompts Tropo activation immediately. Targeted: Tropo announces itself just-in-time when there's actual news.

**Hook 4 — Daily standing-ops via scheduling.**
Tropo registers its desired daily-or-twice-daily cadence via the schedule mechanism (§9). Recurring autonomous runs handle fleet-ops + vault health regardless of user activity. Earns Tropo's daily presence on operational grounds, not just news delivery.

## §9 — Schedule mechanism (L1 portable + harness adapters)

### L1 promise — portable substrate (canonical)

Tropo's desired cadence + work lives in a harness-agnostic registry:

```
.tropo-studio/scheduled-tasks/
└── tropo-schedule.yaml
```

Declares:
- target_agent: `tropo`
- cadence: `daily` (or `every 12 hours`, etc.)
- last_run, next_run_after
- work_units: `[fleet-ops, vault-health, alerts-review, release-news-check]`
- non_interactive: `true` (autonomous mode)

User-initiated check at agent boot — if `last_run < cutoff`, any other agent prompts user to run Tropo. Universal fallback that works in any harness, including ones with no scheduling primitive at all.

### Harness adapters (opt-in accelerators)

Each harness adapter consumes the schedule registry and registers harness-specific scheduling:

**Claude Code adapter (first to ship; current dogfood):**
- Reads `tropo-schedule.yaml`
- Registers `RemoteTrigger` for autonomous wake (server-side execution; user can be away)
- Optionally registers `CronCreate` for in-session opportunistic moments (REPL-idle fires; user is at machine, paused; interactive prompting possible)
- Uses `PushNotification` to bridge autonomous findings to user attention when something is user-actionable

**Other harnesses (future adapters):**
- Codex / Gemini CLI / Cursor adapters implement against equivalent primitives where available
- Harnesses without scheduling primitives: L1 user-initiated check at agent boot is the universal fallback

### Tread-lightly architectural commitment

**RemoteTrigger / CronCreate / PushNotification are Claude Code accelerators, not load-bearing for Tropo's promise.** A stranger running their Studio in a non-Claude-Code harness still gets full Tropo functionality (via Hook 1's universal agent-activation.playbook check + the L1 schedule registry); they just don't get the autonomous-while-away convenience. The L1 substrate stays portable; harness adapters multiply over time as needed.

## §10 — Tropo as a documented subsystem

**The framing:** Tropo (the agent) + its supporting infrastructure (CAPSULE.md governance contract, schedule registry, harness adapters, four-phase first-user playbook, role docs) becomes its own subsystem hub — a peer to the seven existing subsystem hubs (tropo-governance, tropo-work, tropo-agents, tropo-playbooks, tropo-library, tropo-documentation, tropo-rendering).

**Capability membership pattern applies.** Every artifact in the subsystem declares `member_of: <tropo-agent-hub-uid>` — the agent identity files, the CAPSULE.md, the schedule registry, the welcome playbook, the role docs, the harness adapters. The hub's body documents the subsystem coherently as a single architectural piece.

**Subsystem hub name (locked at v0.2 walk): `tropo-link`.**

The naming convention from existing subsystem hubs (tropo-library, tropo-work, tropo-playbooks, tropo-governance, tropo-agents, tropo-rendering, tropo-documentation) is `tropo-<thing-or-process>` — descriptive, not role-personification. Mike-A55 reframe at walk: *"It is a critical linkage between all the federated vaults and home-base/HQ."* Linkage captures the through-line (every role — welcome, news, updates, fleet-ops, standing-ops, help, health — is a form of HQ↔Studio connection). Short form `tropo-link` chosen over `tropo-linkage` for descriptive clarity; "link" is not overloaded in our governed vocabulary (we say `member_of:`, "edges", "graph relations" — never "link" as a named concept).

Subsystem `tropo-link` composes with the federation arc (V41 v1.14 thesis lineage) and with the [Studio-Registration + Studio-Prefixed UIDs federation foundation thesis (`7cac6473`)](7cac6473.md) filed at v0.2 walk for v2.x+ activation.
