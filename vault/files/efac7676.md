## What Went Well

**The fire suppression finding prevented a significant delay.** When the Permit Research Agent flagged that three households required the RC-FS-ADD addendum, that information reached the Vendor Coordination Agent through the channel before vendor selection was finalized. SunPath Solar was chosen specifically because they were RC-FS-ADD certified. If that finding had arrived after the vendor was selected, we would have had to restart the vendor process for three households.

The governance worked exactly as designed: the permit agent found something, posted it to the channel, and the downstream agents incorporated it without the permit agent having to touch their files.

**The readiness gate prevented a scheduling conflict.** Household 06 was unavailable until April 20. Because the Customer Onboarding Agent hadn't posted a `HOUSEHOLD READY` event for them, the Vendor Coordination Agent held their installation window. When household 06 signed on April 21, the window was scheduled without conflict. No household was contacted by an installer before they were ready.

**Generational continuity held.** The Permit Research Agent retired after session 2 and a new generation activated. The living transfer was complete. The successor knew what had been filed, what was pending, and what the county had flagged. There was no loss of context. The pilot continued without the human having to re-brief the agent.

---

## What to Improve for the Next Pilot

**The addendum explanation call (household 09) took three days.** The Customer Onboarding Agent was waiting for the household to respond. A better design would include a follow-up protocol in the agent charter — if no response within 48 hours, escalate with a phone call template rather than waiting passively.

**One finding for the next pilot's design brief:**

> The agents coordinated well. The humans reviewed faster than expected because the coordination channel made the status of every household immediately legible. The bottleneck in the next pilot will not be agent coordination — it will be human review capacity. Design Gate 5 to batch approvals rather than reviewing one household at a time.
