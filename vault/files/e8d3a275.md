## What Mike said (2026-05-16 morning, brainstorm session)

> "I'm going to walk them with you. I want you to capture all this in a note and put it in web-pipeline inbox.
>
> I want to expand what we are doing with the website. I have been pushing for a launch of our substack articles. The website was going to be where we pointed people to download tropo and start building with their own studio. That is still valid, but I want to the website to be much more — the hub of learning and community on the new agentic way of building.
>
> We have developed a new way of working between humans and agents, and we have the 'receipts' to back it up.
>
> One challenge I am probably underestimating is how difficult it will be for people to understand agents, work pipelines, graph-based file systems, tools. It's a lot. It's hard to do when you are working in a studio for the first time.
>
> However, websites are made for humans. We can make it interactive with graphics, training videos, and built-in agents on the site that answer questions about how things work. We can publish all our sub-systems on the site with visuals that will help them understand how it all works.
>
> Also, why should we educate the world alone? We could have a community section where people can ask questions and others can answer. I know, kind of like reddit, but for our topic of tropo and agentic building.
>
> Our ultimate goal here is building a community and the website is the hub for that."

## The reframe in plain terms

The tropo-ai.com website was scoped (per the [Launch Foundations project plan (6d4f7a82)](6d4f7a82.md)) as a **marketing-conversion surface** — front door, blog mirror from Substack, download point for Tropo-OS, an agent-builder wizard for engagement + email capture.

Mike is now expanding the scope to **a community + learning hub**:

- The website is the **public face of Tropo's "new way of working"** — not just a place to convert visitors into downloaders, but the place where the world goes to *understand* the agentic-building method.
- **Education becomes a primary surface**, not a secondary one. Interactive explanations of agents, work pipelines, graph-based file systems, tools, subsystems — with visuals, training videos, and embedded AI assistants that can answer questions about Tropo using Tropo as their substrate.
- **Community becomes the long-term goal.** A space where Tropo users help each other learn — Reddit-shaped, but focused on agentic building. The community is the ultimate moat; the hub is the convening place.

The agent-builder wizard discussion we were having just before this note (full Studio bundle vs intent file) becomes one feature of many, not the centerpiece. The centerpiece is the *hub experience itself*.

## What this changes vs the prior plan

### Stays the same
- `tropo-ai.com` as the domain
- The Tropo brand (bone/ember, "operating system for human-AI teams," Substack-as-Workshop voice)
- The current placeholder we shipped 2026-05-15 — but as a temporary stand-in for the eventual hub homepage
- The Tropo platform (Mo Author, KB resolver, etc.) in `tropo-ai/tropo-ai` — unchanged; the website is separate
- Argo-OS as the operational Studio — unchanged
- Apache 2.0 license decision — unchanged (OS license is separate from website code license)
- Vercel as the host
- The decision to keep the website in a separate repo (`tropo-ai/website`)
- Web-pipeline as the deploy mechanism

### Changes
- **Scope of website itself.** From ~5-10 marketing pages to a sustained content + community surface with many sections.
- **The agent-builder's role.** Was the centerpiece engagement feature; becomes one of several surfaces.
- **Content production discipline.** From "Substack mirror + occasional update" to "ongoing content production with cadence" — articles, tutorials, visual subsystem explainers, training videos, embedded AI walkthroughs.
- **Engineering surface area.** From "static-ish marketing site" to "real web application with: user accounts (for community), content storage, search, moderation, possibly forums, embedded AI assistants."
- **Cadence of web-pipeline.** Was scoped for "per-deploy one ship-event"; now needs to handle ongoing content publishing (which may not be deploy-shaped).
- **The Substack relationship.** Currently positioned as the canonical authoring surface with the website as mirror. May need to evolve — if the website becomes the learning hub, native authoring + cross-posting to Substack may make more sense than the reverse.
- **The team requirements.** Eventually needs: content writers, illustrators, video producers, community moderators. Not at v1, but they're on the horizon.

## Talos T4 honest reactions

**Where this lands strong:**

1. **It matches the masthead article's claims.** "From Chatbot to Operating System" is positioning Tropo as a category-defining product. Category-defining products need category-defining homes on the web. Stripe.com, Vercel.com, HashiCorp.com — every category-defining tech company has a hub-shaped website, not just a marketing page. The expanded scope matches the ambition.

2. **The "embedded AI assistants on the site" idea is recursive proof.** If a visitor lands on the site and asks "what's a capsule?" and the AI assistant — running on Tropo's own substrate — answers correctly, the medium IS the message. Tropo's substrate explaining Tropo's substrate is the strongest possible demo.

3. **Community as the long-term moat is correct strategy.** Open-source projects compound through community. The framework + license + content combine into a community; the community is what makes the project resilient over years. Discord/Reddit/forum-style spaces are how the open-source-community pattern actually grows.

4. **The learning gap is real.** Agentic building IS hard to understand from a fresh Studio. The Substack article explains it well in prose, but prose has limits. Interactive subsystem visualizers, agent-flow diagrams, capsule-graph explorers — these are real product surfaces that pay off for genuinely-confused users.

**Where I'd push back / surface considerations:**

1. **This is 5x the engineering scope of the original plan.** The current Launch Foundations project plan ([6d4f7a82](6d4f7a82.md)) targets ~10-14 hours total. A community + learning hub at the scope Mike's describing is closer to ~200-400 hours of engineering + ongoing operational work. Worth being explicit: this is a much bigger commitment. Phasing matters.

2. **Community is hard to maintain.** Reddit-shaped surfaces require moderation, anti-spam, abuse handling, ongoing community management. They become a real operational cost. Many open-source communities start with a forum/Discord and never reach critical mass; the surface drains the founder's attention without delivering. **Suggestion: don't ship community at v1. Validate audience interest via Substack subscriber count + downloads + email-capture before investing in community infrastructure.**

3. **Content production has a cadence problem.** "Articles + visuals + training videos + interactive walkthroughs" implies a content factory. Mike is currently producing Substack pieces with Metis at a rate of ~1 per few weeks. Scaling to "we run a learning hub" means either more content velocity (more time investment) or more contributors (which requires community first, which requires content first — chicken-and-egg).

4. **The "embedded AI assistant on the site" is genuinely hard.** Beautiful in theory, fragile in practice. Token costs scale with site traffic. Hallucination risk on technical-detail questions damages credibility (the worst kind of risk for a learning hub). Mitigations exist (RAG against the vault, tight system prompts, guardrails) but each adds complexity. **Worth a separate brief on its own.**

5. **The Substack-as-canonical decision may need to flip.** If the website is the hub, authoring directly there starts to make more sense than authoring on Substack and mirroring. But that contradicts the prior brief [4e9f7a23](4e9f7a23.md). Open question to revisit.

**Where I think the right phasing lives:**

This isn't a v1-launch decision. It's a multi-cycle horizon:

- **Phase 0 (already done):** Placeholder live on tropo-ai.com.
- **Phase 1 (the current Launch Foundations project, in flight):** Marketing-conversion surface — blog mirror, download surface, agent-builder wizard, email capture. Ship in ~4-8 weeks.
- **Phase 2 (next horizon, 3-6 months out):** Begin learning-hub content. Native subsystem explainers with visuals. First training videos. Maybe an embedded AI assistant in beta. Substack stays primary authoring; website hosts both mirror and native long-form learning content.
- **Phase 3 (6-12 months out):** Community surface. Forum-style or Discord-style (the right tool here is its own brief). Native authoring becomes possible (CMS or markdown-in-repo). User accounts. Real moderation tooling.
- **Phase 4 (12-24 months out):** The full hub vision. Multi-section site, embedded AI throughout, mature community, content velocity sustained by community contributors.

**What this means for the in-flight project:**

The current [Launch Foundations project (2c8e93f1)](2c8e93f1.md) doesn't need to change. It's still the right v1 scope. What changes is that we author *toward* the hub vision, not against it — design decisions in the current project should be made aware that the website will grow into a learning hub.

Specifically:
- The agent-builder brief (Deliverable #8, not yet authored) — design it as one of several surfaces, not THE centerpiece. The wizard's narrative should leave room for "and here are other ways to learn about Tropo."
- The Substack RSS pipeline (Deliverable #9, authored, locked open question on cadence) — still right at v1. May evolve as native authoring becomes possible in Phase 2.
- The GitHub Releases integration (Deliverable #10, authored, license decision now locked Apache 2.0) — still right at v1.
- The placeholder page eventually evolves into the hub homepage — design it accordingly.

## Open questions surfaced (not resolved)

1. **Phasing commitment.** Does Mike want the website to architect *toward* the hub from v1 onward, or is he content to ship v1 marketing-conversion-shaped and revisit the hub vision in Phase 2?
2. **The embedded AI assistant.** When? RAG-against-vault? Anti-hallucination strategy? Token economics at scale? Worth its own design brief.
3. **Community substrate choice.** Custom-built (high cost), Discord (vendor lock + Discord-shaped UX), Discourse (real forum, self-hosted), GitHub Discussions (lightweight, requires GitHub account), something else? Phase 3+ decision.
4. **Substack-as-canonical vs website-as-canonical.** Affects content authoring flow. Phase 2 question.
5. **Visual production capacity.** Who creates the subsystem visualizers, training videos, illustrations? Mike + Metis? Outside contractors? Community contributions? Phase 2+ question.
6. **Native long-form content authoring tools.** If the website hosts native articles, what's the authoring flow? Markdown-in-repo (engineer-shaped)? CMS (designer-shaped)? Both? Phase 2 question.
