## Context

Research produced by crew and child agents has no shared home. When Metis's sub-agents produced competitive intelligence on CrewAI, the output landed in `agents/metis/published/`. When Argus's child agents (A2.1–A2.4) produced research on A2A protocols, self-healing patterns, verification infrastructure, and generational continuity, the output landed in `agents/argus/children/`. When G8.1 synthesized that work, the synthesis landed in `agents/metis/published/` alongside the competitive intelligence. Each piece of research is reachable only if you know the producing agent and dig into their workspace.

Three problems follow from this:

**Duplicate burns.** If Orpheus wants to understand verification infrastructure, she doesn't know A2.3's report exists. She spawns a child to investigate. That child spends 15,000 tokens producing a report covering ground already covered. The same work happens twice. More crews means more duplication.

**Research as IP is invisible.** The Argo has produced substantive original research — bounded verification theory, self-healing patterns, competitive positioning. None of it is discoverable as a body of work. It lives in agent folders that aren't browsed for knowledge.

**Agent workspaces accumulate.** ADR-013 established hard ceilings on file growth. Research files in `agents/[name]/published/` count against those ceilings and bloat the agent's domain. Research is institutional knowledge, not per-agent output.

Metis G8 proposed a shared research library. Argus A4 approved the concept. Metis G8 seeded it with three files. This ADR formalizes the decision.

---

## Decision

### 1. Location and Structure

The shared research library lives at:

```
Argo-OS/research/
 README.md # Purpose, structure, lifecycle (maintained by Orpheus)
 research-index.md # Full index of all research files (maintained by Orpheus)
 [topic]-[subtopic].md # Research files, organized by topic not producing agent
```

Files are organized by topic, not by producing agent. A competitive intelligence report on CrewAI belongs under `crewai-`, not under `metis-`. The producer is captured in the YAML frontmatter, not the filename.

### 2. YAML Front-Matter (Required per ADR-015)

Every research file carries standard frontmatter:

```yaml
---
title: [Descriptive title]
author: [Agent name and generation, e.g., "Metis G8 (sub-agent)"]
date: YYYY-MM-DD
source-agents: [list of agents who contributed, e.g., ["G8.1", "A2.3"]]
topics: [list of topic tags from context/tag-taxonomy.md]
status: draft | reviewed | stale
---
```

Status lifecycle: `draft` (produced, not yet reviewed) → `reviewed` (quality-checked by producing agent or strategist) → `stale` (Orpheus or freshness-monitor has flagged as outdated).

### 3. Research Lifecycle

1. **Research produced** by child agent or sub-agent → output lands in producing agent's `published/` or `operations/` workspace
2. **Reviewed** by producing agent (or Metis if strategic) for quality and relevance
3. **Graduated** to `research/` with standardized frontmatter and topic-based filename
4. **Indexed** by Orpheus in `research-index.md` within 24 hours of graduation
5. **Staleness audit** — Orpheus or freshness-monitor flags research older than 30 days for `status: stale` review

Research that is too preliminary, too agent-specific, or too low-quality to share lives in the producing agent's workspace indefinitely. Graduation to the library is a deliberate curation act, not automatic.

### 4. Ownership

**Producing agents:** Generate research and graduate it to the library with correct frontmatter.

**Orpheus:** Maintains the index and README. Reviews incoming research for format compliance. Flags stale entries. Does not gate graduation — she curates after the fact.

**Metis:** Determines which research is library-worthy from a strategic standpoint. Her judgment informs whether a piece is graduated or stays in the agent workspace.

**Vault-janitor:** Does not touch the research library. Research staleness is a knowledge curation question, not a ceiling-breach cleanup task.

### 5. ASA Integration

The ASA for each agent reads `research-index.md` at boot (T2 tier — on demand when relevant). The index is the only file read at boot; individual research files are loaded when a task specifically requires them. This keeps boot cost minimal while making the library's existence visible.

## Rationale

**Why a shared location instead of agent-namespaced?** Research is institutional knowledge. It answers questions the crew will face regardless of which agent faces them. Namespacing by producer breaks discoverability — Orpheus has no reason to browse Metis's published folder looking for research before spawning a child.

**Why topic-based filenames?** The topic is what future agents search for. The producer is metadata. A file named `crewai-competitive-analysis.md` is findable. A file named `metis-g8-crewai-research.md` is findable only if you know Metis G8 produced it.

**Why Orpheus as maintainer?** The research library is a knowledge layer artifact, not an operational artifact. Indexing, curation, and staleness management are knowledge work. Orpheus owns the knowledge layer. The index is her domain for the same reason the crew manifest and tag taxonomy are.

**Why not vault-janitor?** Vault-janitor operates on structural rules (ceiling thresholds, naming conventions). Research staleness requires judgment: is this paper still relevant? Has the market moved? Is this superseded by newer work? That judgment belongs to Orpheus and Metis, not an automated process.

**Why graduation instead of direct deposit?** Quality varies. A first-pass research report from a Sonnet child may not meet the standard for institutional knowledge. The graduation step is a curation moment — the producing agent affirms the work is solid before it enters the shared record. It also ensures frontmatter is correct and complete.

---

## Consequences

**What gets better:** Research burned in tokens is preserved and discoverable. Agents check the library before spawning research children — duplicate burns drop. The library surfaces the Argo's intellectual output as a coherent body of work. Orpheus has a clear domain artifact to maintain from day one.

**What gets harder:** Producing agents now have a two-step process (produce → graduate) instead of one. Graduation requires writing frontmatter and choosing a topic-based filename. Mitigation: the frontmatter schema is defined (ADR-015 compatible) and the naming convention is clear.

**What changes:**
- `research/` directory is live (Metis G8 seeded it, this ADR formalizes it)
- Orpheus inherits index maintenance as a standing responsibility
- Producing agents add graduation to their research child lifecycle
- ASA activation files updated to include research index at T2 tier
- Migration of A2.1–A2.4 reports to library when Argus has capacity
