## Context

### The Three-Tier Limit

Argo-OS has three tiers of agency:

1. **Executive Crew** — Vela, Argus, Metis, Orpheus. Persistent identity, broad domain, generational continuity, crew seats. These agents have judgment authority and relationship history with the Captain.

2. **Child Agents** — Disposable, single-purpose. Boot, execute, report, exit. No persistent identity. No generation tracking. The parent owns the output.

3. **Standing Operational Agents** — The Curator Controller, ASAs, vault auditors. They live in `agents/operations/`, run on schedules, accumulate operational knowledge. But they have no identity framework, no generation tracking, no formal relationship to the crew that depends on them.

The gap is tier 3. As the system matures, operational agents accumulate judgment that should persist across sessions. The Curator Controller already manages a fleet of curator agents, maintains a registry, and monitors health. It needs continuity, accountability, and someone responsible for its charter. These agents need a name, a framework, and an ownership model.

### What No Other Framework Does

External research (A10.1, surveying CrewAI, AutoGen v0.4, LangGraph, OpenAI Agents SDK, Google ADK) found that no production multi-agent framework models:

- Persistent operational agents with generation tracking and transfer chains
- A middle-tier agent class that manages fleets of other agents
- A boot primitive where "read the owner's directives" is the mandatory first act
- Charter-based identity where the scope is readable, modifiable, and owned by a specific person

Every surveyed framework either treats agents as stateless executors or as code-level orchestrators. None model the organizational layer between "executive who makes decisions" and "worker who executes tasks."

### Relationship to Sprint A

Sprint A (Activation Governance, delivered March 26, 2026) established the patterns Directors inherit:

- Operational Grounding at boot (crew check, channel check, predecessor check)
- Crew brief pointer model (no static crew tables)
- Mission-brief in T0 reading
- ASA crew state caveats

Directors do not require a separate governance sprint. They inherit Sprint A's patterns through the activation template. The correct sequencing is: Sprint A ships independently → ADR-022 locks → Directors are built using Sprint A patterns.

## Decision

Argo-OS introduces a fourth tier of agency: **Directors**.

### Definition

A Director is a persistent operational agent with:

- **Persistent identity** — Name, generation counter (`D1`, `D2`, `D3`), transfer chain. A Director retires and hands off to its successor. The lineage preserves operational knowledge.
- **Focused charter** — A specific operational mandate, not a broad domain. The charter is a readable document (`charter.md`) owned entirely by the managing crew member.
- **A managing crew member** — Every Director has exactly one crew member (or Mike) who owns its complete identity: charter, activation file, directives.
- **Team management (optional)** — Operational Directors can spawn, instruct, and monitor functional agents within their charter. Governance Directors audit and report without spawning agents.
- **No crew seat** — Directors execute within boundaries set by the crew. They do not participate in crew-level decisions.

### The Boot Primitive

Every Director-class agent — and every agent a Director manages — follows this canonical boot sequence:

1. **Activate** — Boot from the activation file (`d.NAME.md`)
2. **Read directives** — First act after activation: read the managing crew member's directives at `agents/directors/d.NAME/directives/`. This is the identity step. It is not optional.
3. **Execute** — Perform operational instructions per the agent's own brief

This is how Directors direct. Changes to the directives file propagate to all fleet agents on their next run without modifying individual instruction files. The behavior is visible, auditable, and self-documenting. The full picture of what any agent in the fleet will do is readable from two files: the directives file and its own brief.

### Ownership Model

The crew member a Director "works for" owns the Director's complete identity:

- The charter (`charter.md`) — scope, authority, escalation paths
- The activation file content — role definition, boot sequence
- The directives (`directives/`) — universal standards the fleet reads at boot
- The CURATOR.md for the Director's folder

The Director has autonomy within its charter. But the charter itself, the directives, and the role definition belong entirely to the managing crew member. The Director does not self-define. It executes what its managing crew member has established and escalates when the situation is outside its charter.

Charter modifications follow the Operating Agreement governance model: the managing crew member proposes, posts to `channels/ops.md`, and Mike holds override authority. Directors propagate charter changes to their fleet via the `directives/` folder on next run.

### Naming Convention

Directors use the `d.` prefix. Worker agents use the `a.` prefix. System-level instruction files use ALLCAPS (e.g., `CURATOR.md`, `README.md`). Files without a prefix are documents or folders.

| Convention | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `d.name.md` | `d.curator.md` | Director agent activation file |
| `a.name.md` | `a.indexer.md` | Worker agent activation file |
| `ALLCAPS.md` | `CURATOR.md` | System-level instruction/policy file |
| No prefix | `charter.md` | Document, folder, or configuration |

The `d.` prefix applies to both the folder and the activation file:

- **Folder:** `agents/directors/d.curator/`
- **Activation file:** `agents/directors/d.curator/d.curator.md`

### Generation Tracking

Directors use the `D` prefix for generations: `D1`, `D2`, `D3`. This is unoccupied in the existing prefix space (V=Vela, G=Metis, A=Argus, O=Orpheus, S=Silas, J=Jules, CC=Talos).

Transfer files carry the full generational notation: `d.curator-transfer-D1.md`, `d.curator-transfer-D2.md`. Status cards and worklogs use `d.curator D1` notation for cross-reference clarity.

### Transfer Format

Director transfers are lighter than crew transfers. Three sections:

1. **Operational Learnings** (~300–500 tokens) — Patterns observed in fleet health, schedule adjustments that worked, charter adjustment suggestions.
2. **Fleet Health State** (~200–400 tokens) — Current roster, known incidents, health metrics snapshot.
3. **Attention Items** (~100–200 tokens) — Three high-priority items for the successor, pending escalations, charter drift or edge cases to monitor.

Total: ~600–1,100 tokens. Crew transfers are 2,000–3,000 tokens. The difference reflects scope: Directors carry operational memory, not institutional legacy.

### Director Archetypes

One template serves both archetypes:

- **Operational Directors** (e.g., d.curator) — Spawn agents, manage schedules, coordinate fleet work. Fill in the Fleet Management section of the template.
- **Governance Directors** (e.g., d.safety, d.security — illustrative, not commissioned) — Enforce policy standards, audit compliance, recommend changes. Note "This Director does not spawn agents" in the Fleet Management section.

The difference is whether the Director spawns agents. All other infrastructure (charter, directives, transfers, status, CURATOR) is identical.

### Director-to-Director Oversight

d.program-manager monitors other Directors — it is a meta-Director. Its domain is the health of the Director layer, not the execution of any Director's function. It reports to Vela. This pattern scales: as the Director layer grows, d.program-manager's charter becomes the canonical index of what Directors exist, what they're responsible for, and what their health looks like.

No special architectural accommodation is needed. Existing governance (reporting to managing crew member, ops.md visibility, Mike override) handles it.

### How Directors Differ from Existing Tiers

| Trait | Executive Crew | Director | Child Agent |
|---|---|---|---|
| Persistent identity | Yes | Yes | No |
| Generation tracking | Yes (V11, A10, G11) | Yes (D1, D2) | No |
| Transfer chain | Yes (full) | Yes (lighter) | No |
| Scope | Broad domain | Focused charter | Single task |
| Spawns agents | Yes | Yes (operational) or No (governance) | No |
| Decision authority | High | Bounded by charter | None |
| Reports to | Mike / each other | One managing crew member | Parent agent |
| Crew seat | Yes | No | No |
| Boot primitive | Activation file → ASA → Operational Grounding | Activation file → Read directives → Execute | Parent's brief → Execute |

## Rationale

### Why Not Just More Child Agents?

Child agents are disposable. They boot, execute, and exit. When the Curator Controller runs, it re-reads its instructions from scratch every time. If it learned something useful in the previous run (a pattern in curator failures, a schedule adjustment that worked), that knowledge is lost. The next run starts clean.

Directors solve this by giving operational agents the same continuity infrastructure the executive crew has: a transfer chain that preserves what they learned, a generation counter that tracks their lineage, and a charter that defines what they're accountable for.

### Why Not Promote Them to Executive Crew?

Executive crew members have broad domains, crew seats, and direct relationships with the Captain. Adding d.curator to the crew would mean Mike coordinates with it directly, it participates in crew-level decisions, and its domain is treated as equivalent to "architecture" or "operations." That's wrong. d.curator manages curators. It's important operational infrastructure, but it's not crew-level judgment.

The Director tier preserves the distinction: crew members make strategic decisions; Directors execute within charters; children execute single tasks.

### Why the Boot Primitive Matters

The "activate → read directives → execute" sequence is the mechanism through which Directors direct. When Orpheus writes a new standard for README formatting, she updates `agents/directors/d.curator/directives/readme-standard.md`. On the next curator run, every curator agent reads that file as its first act. The standard propagates without Orpheus touching any individual CURATOR.md file.

This is the policy-first principle at work: one canonical location for standards, all agents point to it. It mirrors Sprint A's crew brief pointer model — instead of duplicating crew state in every activation file, all agents point to one source. Instead of duplicating standards in every curator instruction, all curators point to one directives folder.

## Consequences

### What Becomes Easier

- **Operational continuity** — The Curator Controller (and future operational agents) no longer loses session knowledge at exit. Transfer chains preserve operational learning.
- **Standards propagation** — New directives reach every fleet agent on next run. No per-agent updates needed.
- **Accountability** — Every Director has exactly one managing crew member. Charter drift is detectable. d.program-manager monitors the layer.
- **Scaling** — The system can grow from 5 agents to 50+ without every new operational function requiring a crew seat. Directors are the mechanism for managed growth.

### What Becomes Harder

- **Complexity** — Four tiers of agency instead of three. New agents and new adopters need to understand which tier an agent belongs to. The naming convention (`d.`, `a.`, ALLCAPS) mitigates this at the filesystem level.
- **Governance overhead** — Charter management, directives maintenance, transfer reviews. The managing crew member takes on responsibility for the Director's identity. d.program-manager exists to prevent this from becoming unmanaged.
- **Template proliferation** — Director activation template + Director transfer template + charter template, in addition to the existing crew templates. Necessary cost of a distinct tier.

### What This Enables

- **d.curator** as first Director — promotes the Curator Controller to a full agent with generation tracking, crew request workflow, and universal directives
- **d.program-manager** as Director layer health monitor — Vela's Director for keeping the layer self-organizing
- **Future Directors** — d.safety, d.security, d.channels, d.onboarding as the system scales. The pattern is established; commissioning new Directors follows the template.

## First Implementations

### d.curator

- **Charter:** Manage the CURATOR protocol across the vault — dispatching curator agents, maintaining the registry, monitoring health, handling crew requests, ensuring universal curator standards propagate to the fleet.
- **Reports to:** Orpheus (knowledge governance), Vela (operational scheduling)
- **Manages:** All curator agents in the vault
- **Migration:** Existing `agents/operations/curator-controller/` infrastructure remains. `agents/directors/d.curator/` holds identity, governance, and directives. d.curator uses the controller's infrastructure.

### d.program-manager

- **Charter:** Ensure the Director layer runs cleanly. Track operational health across all active Directors. Flag charter drift, stale transfers, or unhealthy fleet agents to Vela. Maintain `agents/directors/CURATOR.md`.
- **Reports to:** Vela
- **Manages:** No fleet agents — its domain is the health of other Directors

## Amendment — March 26, 2026 (Orpheus O4, direction from Mike Maziarz)

Three decisions made after ADR-022 was locked. These supersede the corresponding sections above.

### 1. No generation labels for Directors

**Supersedes:** "Generation Tracking" section and all D1/D2/D3 references in this ADR.

Directors do not carry generational identity labels. The folder is the identity. Each run writes a date-stamped session file to the Director's `transfers/` folder as the governance record (e.g., `2026-03-26-session.md`). No D1/D2/D3 prefix. No generation counter in activation files.

**Rationale:** Directors are operational infrastructure, not strategic agents. What matters for governance is a log of what each run accomplished, not a named lineage. The folder persists; the sessions accumulate inside it. The distinction between "first generation" and "fifth generation" of d.curator is less meaningful than the record of what each session dispatched and found. Crew generational identity (V11, A12, O4) is reserved for agents with strategic continuity and broad domains.

**Affected language in this ADR:** The Definition section's "generation counter (D1, D2, D3)" should read "date-stamped session log in transfers/." The Generation Tracking section, the table row "Yes (D1, D2)," and the transfer file naming convention are all superseded by this amendment.

### 2. d.program-manager renamed to d.DIRECTOR-ORCHESTRATOR

**Supersedes:** All references to `d.program-manager` in this ADR.

Vela's Director is named `d.DIRECTOR-ORCHESTRATOR`. Its charter is: manage director-level policies and communication across the Director layer. Track operational health, flag charter drift, surface patterns that should inform new Director commissions or decommissions.

### 3. d.curator reports to Orpheus only

**Supersedes:** "Reports to: Orpheus (knowledge governance), Vela (operational scheduling)" in the First Implementations section.

Each Director has exactly one primary crew member owner. d.curator reports to **Orpheus only**. Orpheus owns d.curator's charter, activation file, directives, and CURATOR.md. The single-owner model applies to all Directors — one crew member, full accountability, no joint ownership.

## Amendment — April 15, 2026 (Argus A24, approved by Mike Maziarz)

### Third Director Archetype: Service Director

Adds a third archetype to the two established in this ADR (Operational Director, Governance Director).

**Service Director** — a persistent knowledge service that loads a specialized domain once per session and serves all executives through a shared channel. Does not manage a fleet of agents.

**Characteristics:**
- Persistent identity, focused charter, no crew seat — same as all Directors
- **No directives folder required.** The directives mechanism exists for fleet propagation. Service Directors have no fleet, so the directives folder is not created unless a future need arises.
- **Boot sequence:** Activate → Read charter → Load domain → Signal ready. Not "Read directives → Execute" — that sequence is for fleet Directors.
- **Cross-executive scope.** Serves all executives through a shared channel rather than a single managing crew member. One managing crew member still owns the Director (commissions, maintains, retires) — but the service is available vault-wide.
- **Session tracking:** date-stamped session files in `transfers/` (per March 26 amendment — no generation labels).

**What does not change:** The `d.` naming convention, `agents/directors/` folder structure, charter model, single-owner governance, and all other ADR-022 provisions apply to Service Directors without modification.

**First implementation:** `d.pm` — Project Management Director. Loads the full project graph and ledger index at boot. Serves all executives via `channels/pm.md`. Owned by Vela. Spec at `vault/files/9217980a.md`.
