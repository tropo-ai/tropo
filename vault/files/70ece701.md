## Toy-Content Specification

**What the project produces at Build stage:** a 1-page content brief (~500 words of publication-ready markdown) for a hypothetical HR analytics SaaS named **Helm**, targeted at mid-market HR leaders (200–2,000 employee companies).

**Differentiator:** 90-day retention signal — catches turnover risk 90 days earlier than quarterly surveys.

**Primary format:** 1-page brief suitable for publication in HR industry publications (SHRM, HR Executive), ~500 words.

**Voice:** professional, HR-industry-appropriate. Generic numbers (e.g., "67% mid-market turnover within first year"). No real competitor names — generic references to "quarterly survey platforms" and "legacy HR tools." CTA: "See a live demo." No real URL.

**Use cases (three):**
1. Hiring manager retention alert — flag when a new hire's engagement signal dips in the first 90 days
2. HR leader team-health dashboard — rolling view of team-level retention signal across the org
3. Executive compensation strategy signal — identify compensation-adjacent retention risks at the comp-cycle review

**Scope notes for the walker:** the Helm SaaS is fictional; don't make the content perfect — the walk is the point. Good-enough professional copy is the target. Content continuity across stages (the same Helm thesis threads through concept → design-brief → arch-spec → build → release) is the substantive test.

This project exists for one reason: to be walked. sa.pipeline-walker [a71ace01](a71ace01.md) needs a stable, small, fictional-but-coherent target to walk end-to-end. Real crew projects are too large and too entangled for a demonstration walk; a toy project is purpose-built.

Verification-first identity made tangible in 20 minutes.

**The Helm content-brief substance is incidental to the walk's value.** The value is: does the walker produce 5 typed artifacts that compose through `derived_from:` edges? Does position advance monotonically forward? Does each stage's GATE decision declare cleanly? A stranger auditing the receipt can answer all three from the filesystem alone. That's the demonstration.
