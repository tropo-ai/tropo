## Purpose

Optional pause point where the draft can be routed to a designated reviewer before finalization. Pipeline-runs advance through this step WITHOUT ceremony when no reviewer is designated on the activation root. The pipeline does not dictate review criteria — the author or designated reviewer applies whatever criteria matters for THIS piece (voice, accuracy, completeness, argument coherence, etc.).

Per [design brief d3a5f1b9](d3a5f1b9.md) Q1: "Pipelines govern process; capsules govern content. Voice and structure live in artifact capsules (when they exist), not in the pipeline."

## Structure

Leaf step. No children.

## Nodes

No child nodes. Leaf WorkflowNode.

## Flow Rules

Completes via two paths:

- **No reviewer designated.** Activation root's `reviewer:` field is null/absent. Step closes immediately when entered. Pipeline advances to finalize (2e9c4f51).
- **Reviewer designated.** Activation root's `reviewer:` field names a reviewer. Executor waits for an external "review complete" signal (mechanism TBD per Known Enforcement Gaps; convention at v1.0.0). When the signal lands, step closes; pipeline advances to finalize.

Advances to: finalize (2e9c4f51) in either path.

## Cold-Boot Walk-Through

The executor checks the activation root's `reviewer:` field. If null/absent: step closes immediately, pipeline advances to step-5. If a reviewer is named: the draft is routed to them externally (channel post, direct message, whatever the conventional mechanism is); they read it; they signal "review complete" externally; the executor reads that signal and advances. Review feedback is incorporated by the author at step-5 (finalize), not at this step.

## Known Enforcement Gaps

Optional-step semantics are convention-only at v1.0.0. The "wait for external review-complete signal" behavior is convention-only at v1.0.0. See root ([e2f4a8c1](e2f4a8c1.md)) for amendment targets if patterns repeat.
