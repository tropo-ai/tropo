## What This Project Is

This is a complete worked example of a real project moving through every stage of the Tropo Innovation Pipeline — from an initial idea to a deployed pilot.

**Green City Energy** is a startup working to make distributed solar power accessible to individual households. Their first project: coordinating a solar installation pilot for ten homes in Ridgewood, a small city neighborhood. The pilot requires permits, vendor selection, customer onboarding, and deployment — all coordinated by governed AI agents.

This example is not hypothetical. Every artifact here — the design brief, the architecture spec, the tasks, the agent charter — is written as if Green City is real. Because the governance model is real. The artifacts show you exactly what Tropo looks like when it's working.

## Why This Domain

Distributed energy coordination cannot afford ungoverned agents. When an agent acts outside its scope — a permit agent making vendor commitments, a vendor agent modifying permit applications — real harm follows. Someone's installation is delayed. A contract conflicts with a permit condition. A household waits.

The governance here is not overhead. It is the product.

## The Three Agents

**Permit Research Agent** — researches local solar permit requirements, tracks application status, flags compliance issues. Write scope: `ridgewood/permits/` only. Cannot touch vendor or customer files.

**Vendor Coordination Agent** — manages vendor shortlist, tracks quotes, coordinates installation timelines. Write scope: `ridgewood/vendors/` only. Cannot commit to customer timelines without Customer Onboarding Agent confirmation.

**Customer Onboarding Agent** — manages household relationships, tracks consent and paperwork, coordinates the customer-facing experience. Write scope: `ridgewood/customers/` only. Cannot modify permit applications or vendor contracts.

Three agents. Three declared scopes. Nothing falls through the cracks. Nothing gets touched by the wrong agent.
