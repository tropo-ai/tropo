## Intent

A single agent is useful. A team of agents with defined roles, boundaries, and coordination is powerful. This article teaches how teams compose agents into stable working units in Tropo, how the operating agreement governs the team, and how membership persists across sessions. Read this when scaling beyond a solo agent or when the work demands explicit coordination between specialists.

## The Operating Agreement

Every vault has an operating agreement — the constitution that defines:
- The human owner holds final authority
- Agents propose, humans decide
- No agent can expand its own scope or create other agents without approval

This isn't bureaucracy. It's protection. Your agents have boundaries so they don't step on each other or do things you didn't authorize.

## Multiple Agents

A typical small team might have:
- A **strategist** who helps with positioning and decisions
- An **operations lead** who tracks processes and flags issues
- A **content director** who handles writing and communications
- A **researcher** who gathers and synthesizes information

Each agent has its own file, its own workspace, its own scope. They don't interfere with each other because their boundaries are explicit.

## Channels

Agents coordinate through shared files called channels. The main one is `channels/ops.md` — the system-wide operations log where every significant action is recorded.

As your team grows, you can create channels between specific agents (e.g., `strategist-researcher.md`) for focused coordination. Agents communicate asynchronously — they read what the previous agent wrote and continue the work.

## How Coordination Actually Works

Agents don't run simultaneously. They work in sessions:

1. You activate your strategist. It researches a topic and saves a brief.
2. Later, you activate your content director. It reads the strategist's brief and drafts an article.
3. You review the article, make edits, and save it.

The vault is the coordination layer. Files are the messages. The registry is the index. The ops channel is the activity log. Everything is visible, versionable, and yours.

## Growing Your Team

Start with one agent. Add more when you feel the need. The concierge can help you create new agents at any time. Each new agent gets its own folder, workspace, and scope — governed by the same operating agreement.

There's no limit to how many agents you can have. The system scales with your needs.
