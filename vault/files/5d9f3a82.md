*Honest positioning. What others do, what we do, and what the tradeoffs are.*

## The Landscape

There are three ways to build with AI agents today. Tropo occupies a space none of them cover.

### No-Code Agent Platforms (Lindy, Zapier AI, Relevance AI, Notion AI)

**What they do:** Let you build individual AI agents or automations through a visual interface. Point, click, connect.

**What they're good at:** Fast setup. Single-agent tasks. Automating workflows.

**What they don't do:** They build individual agents, not governed teams. No operating agreement, no agent-to-agent coordination, no institutional memory. When you close the tab, the agent forgets everything. And your data lives on their servers.

**Tropo's difference:** Tropo builds a team, not a tool. Your agents have roles, values, scope, and governance. The vault lives on your machine. Nothing is locked into a platform.

### Developer Frameworks (CrewAI, LangGraph, AutoGen, OpenAI Agents SDK)

**What they do:** Let developers write code that orchestrates multiple AI agents.

**What they're good at:** Powerful, flexible, unlimited customization.

**What they don't do:** They require engineering. Python, APIs, infrastructure. If you don't write code, you can't use them.

**Tropo's difference:** No code. A Tropo vault is markdown files in a folder. If you can describe how you work, you can build agents. Developers use frameworks. Everyone else uses Tropo.

### Consulting Firms (McKinsey, Accenture, boutique AI consultants)

**What they do:** Build custom AI solutions for enterprises. Six-figure engagements.

**What they're good at:** Deep expertise, custom implementations, enterprise scale.

**What they don't do:** Serve small teams or solo founders. A 10-person company doesn't have $200K for an AI implementation.

**Tropo's difference:** Free and self-service. Download the zip, run the setup, have a working agent team in 10 minutes. No budget required.

## What Makes Tropo Unique

1. **Files are the system.** No database, no server, no API. Your vault is a folder of markdown files. Any AI can read them. Any human can read them. Git can version them. You own them completely.

2. **Governance through language.** Every folder has rules. Every agent has a scope. The operating agreement defines authority. And it all works because AI agents can read and follow instructions — no code enforcement required. This is a new approach that didn't exist before LLMs.

3. **Model-agnostic.** Your vault works with Claude, GPT, Gemini, or whatever comes next. You are not locked into any AI provider.

4. **Agents remember.** Because work is saved in the vault, agents can pick up where they left off. This is institutional memory — not because the AI has perfect recall, but because the files are always there to read.

5. **Portable.** Your vault is a folder on your computer. Move it anywhere. Back it up. Put it in Dropbox. It's yours.

## Honest Tradeoffs

- **Tropo requires a folder-aware AI tool.** You can't paste it into ChatGPT's web interface. You need Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, or similar.
- **Governance is advisory, not enforced.** Agents follow instructions because LLMs follow instructions. A determined user could ignore the rules. (This is also true of corporate policy — it works because people follow it, not because it's physically enforced.)
- **It's early.** Tropo-OS is v0.1. The system works and is proven, but the ecosystem is young. You're an early adopter.
