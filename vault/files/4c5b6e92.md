## Purpose

The release stage publishes the finalized draft to its primary surface, syndicates to secondary surfaces, engages reader comments, and amplifies via repost. It is the terminal stage of publish-pipeline. Release ends when the bounded engagement window closes (T+2-3 weeks after primary publication via repost-amplify).

Does NOT govern: post-pipeline-run reader engagement (continues on the published surface itself but is not held open in the pipeline), or follow-on essays that derive from this piece's reader response (those start a new pipeline activation).

## Flow Rules

Linear, forward-only. step-7 (syndicate) typically fires at T+3-5 days after step-6 (publish-primary). step-8 (engage-comments) runs ~24-48 hours after each surface publish. step-9 (repost-amplify) fires at T+2-3 weeks after primary publication and is terminal.

These durations are described in the body and recorded on the activation-root metadata; they are not enforced by schema at v1.0.0.

## Cold-Boot Walk-Through

The pipeline-run enters release stage with the finalized draft locked. step-6 publishes to Substack and records the URL on the activation root. step-7 authors and posts the LinkedIn syndication 3-5 days later, recording its URL. step-8 captures reader engagement in a free-form `type: note` engagement record (member of the activation root). step-9 reposts-with-comment at T+2-3 weeks. When step-9 closes, the pipeline-run terminates and the activation root's `status:` flips to `closed`.

## Known Enforcement Gaps

Engagement-step duration semantics (T+24h, T+2-3w windows) are convention-only at v1.0.0. Per-surface artifact capsules (`pub-substack.capsule`, `pub-linkedin.capsule`) are deferred. See root pipeline ([e2f4a8c1](e2f4a8c1.md) §Known Enforcement Gaps) for full list.
