## Intent

Design the coordination model for three AI agents managing the Ridgewood solar pilot. The agents handle permits, vendors, and customer relationships — separately, with defined handoffs, and without stepping on each other.

The design question: **how do you coordinate agents that must share information without sharing authority?**

---

## The Core Design Decision

Each agent has one domain. It owns that domain completely. It does not enter another agent's domain, even when it has relevant information. Relevant information travels through channels — explicit, logged, governed handoffs. Not through agents touching each other's files.

This is not a technical constraint. It is a design choice. The governance model is what makes the system auditable and recoverable. An agent that stays in its lane can be trusted. An agent that drifts into other lanes — even for good reasons — cannot.

**The principle:** Capability is not authorization. Every agent can read all the files. Only the declared scope determines what it writes.

---

## The Three Agents and Their Domains

### Permit Research Agent
**Purpose:** Own the regulatory layer. Nothing moves forward without permits. Nothing slips past the permit agent.

**Domain:** `ridgewood/permits/`
- Researches county and utility permit requirements for residential solar
- Tracks application status for each household
- Flags compliance issues before they become delays
- Transfers permit findings to the channel for Vendor Coordination to act on

**What it does NOT do:** Select vendors. Contact households. Make commitments about timelines.

**When it retires:** The next Permit Research Agent reads the living transfer and continues. The institutional knowledge — what was researched, what was filed, what is pending, what the county flagged — persists in the Vault.

---

### Vendor Coordination Agent
**Purpose:** Own the vendor layer. Find the right installers, get the best terms, coordinate installation windows.

**Domain:** `ridgewood/vendors/`
- Maintains the shortlist of qualified installers
- Tracks quotes, timelines, and capacity
- Flags vendor issues (equipment delays, scheduling conflicts) to the channel
- Confirms installation windows only after Customer Onboarding confirms household readiness

**What it does NOT do:** Modify permit applications. Make commitments to households. Select vendors without checking permit findings.

**The handoff dependency:** Vendor selection must account for permit findings. The Permit Research Agent posts findings to the channel. The Vendor Coordination Agent reads the channel and incorporates them. The dependency is explicit — and logged.

---

### Customer Onboarding Agent
**Purpose:** Own the household relationship. No household moves forward without their explicit readiness.

**Domain:** `ridgewood/customers/`
- Manages consent documentation and household paperwork
- Tracks readiness status for each of the ten households
- Confirms readiness to the Vendor Coordination Agent before installation windows are locked
- Flags household concerns or delays to the channel

**What it does NOT do:** Modify permit applications. Select or contact vendors. Lock installation windows independently.

**The readiness gate:** No installation window is locked without Customer Onboarding confirmation. The Vendor Coordination Agent waits for the signal. This is the most important coordination dependency in the pilot.

---

## The Coordination Protocol

Agents do not communicate directly. They communicate through the channel — `channels/ridgewood-pilot.md` — an append-only log of coordination events.

**Standard channel events:**
- `PERMIT FINDING: [finding] — action needed by Vendor Coordination`
- `VENDOR UPDATE: [update] — for Customer Onboarding awareness`
- `HOUSEHOLD READY: [household ID] — Customer Onboarding to Vendor Coordination`
- `INSTALLATION CONFIRMED: [date, household ID] — Vendor Coordination to ops log`

Every coordination event is logged. Every dependency is explicit. Nothing happens silently.

---

## The Memory Model

Each agent maintains a living transfer at retirement — a structured handoff document that carries:
- What was researched or coordinated in this session
- What is pending and why
- What the successor needs to know that isn't obvious from the files
- What mistakes or friction the successor should avoid

The successor reads the living transfer before anything else. The institutional knowledge does not reset when the agent retires. It compounds.

---

## What This Design Enables

After the Ridgewood Pilot:
- Every action is traceable — who did what, under whose authority, for which household
- Every finding is preserved — the next permit agent inherits the full research record
- Every handoff is logged — the coordination channel is the audit trail
- The pilot can be audited, recovered, or extended without starting from scratch

That is the governance model. Not overhead. Infrastructure.
