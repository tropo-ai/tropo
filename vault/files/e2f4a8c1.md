## Purpose

Governs content authoring and multi-surface release for the Tropo Studio. Accepts a work-item (a scan, brief, or idea) at status `accepted`, transitions it to `active`, and orchestrates specify → author → release. The pipeline ends when a piece has been published to its primary surface, syndicated to secondary surfaces, engaged with reader comments, and amplified via repost.

Mirrors [dev-pipeline (cd1fcd25)](cd1fcd25.md)'s architectural pattern at the per-piece scale that publishing operates on: each published piece is one activation; each activation produces an activation-root project containing the full cycle's artifacts.

Does NOT govern: ideation or pre-acceptance grooming (those happen in the inbox while the work-item is `new` or `accepted` under requester ownership), book-chapter authoring (deferred to a future `book-pipeline`), per-surface artifact content structure (that lives in future per-surface capsules — `pub-substack.capsule`, `pub-linkedin.capsule`, etc., when those exist).

**Architectural commitment** (per [design brief d3a5f1b9](d3a5f1b9.md)): pipelines govern process; capsules govern content. publish-pipeline orchestrates the publishing flow; it does not dictate voice, format, or per-surface structure. Those are artifact-capsule concerns.

## Flow Rules

Execution is linear and forward-only. Stages advance in order: specify → author → release. Within each stage, steps advance in `next_steps:` order.

**Stage transitions:** each stage's `next_steps:` points to the next stage. The pipeline-run executor completes a stage (all its steps at `done`) before starting the next.

**Step transitions:** within a stage, each step's `next_steps:` points to the next step. The executor advances when the prior step reaches `done`.

**Terminal nodes:** release stage (`next_steps: []`) and repost-amplify step (`next_steps: []`) are terminal. Pipeline-run closes when repost-amplify completes (T+2-3 weeks after primary publication, per the bounded-engagement discipline).

**Branching:** none at v1.0.0. Linear pipeline.

**Optional step:** step-4 (review) is OPTIONAL — pipeline-runs advance through it without ceremony unless the activation root declares a `reviewer:`. If `reviewer:` is null/absent, the step closes immediately. If a reviewer is designated, the executor waits for an external "review complete" signal before advancing to step-5 (finalize). This is implemented via convention at v1.0.0; runtime-pause-when-condition mechanism formalizes in pipeline-run.capsule when the pattern repeats across multiple optional steps.

**Engagement timing:** step-8 (engage-comments) runs for ~24-48 hours after each surface publish (steps 6 + 7). Step-9 (repost-amplify) fires at T+2-3 weeks after primary publication. These durations are described in the body and in the activation root's metadata, not enforced by schema.

## Cold-Boot Walk-Through

A pipeline-run has been started against publish-pipeline v1.0.0. The work-item is a scan note at `status: accepted` (e.g., a Lovelace-style market scan that the author has decided to develop into a Substack essay).

**Step 0 — accept-source:** The executor reads the work-item's current status (`accepted`). It writes an acceptance record onto the work-item's `accepted_by:` array: `accepted_by_uid: <this-run-uid>, date_accepted: <today>`. It sets the work-item's `status:` to `active`. The activation-root project is authored with the piece's working title; the source scan is added as a member via `derived_from:`. The work-item is now in flight.

**Step 1 — define-piece:** The agent or author working the run defines the piece's parameters: angle (what specifically is the argument), audience (who is this for), surface (Substack primary, LinkedIn syndication, others?), length (target word count), success criteria (what does "good" look like for this piece). These are captured as metadata on the activation-root frontmatter or as a short define-piece document member of the activation root.

**Step 2 — outline:** The author produces an outline document — structure, claim, evidence chain. Member of the activation root. The outline references back to the source scan(s) via `derived_from:` chain. When the outline is committed, step-2 closes; specify stage advances to author.

**Step 3 — draft:** The author produces the full primary-surface draft (typically a Substack essay). Authored as a `type: document` artifact, member of the activation root. When the draft is committed, step-3 closes.

**Step 4 — review (OPTIONAL):** If the activation root's `reviewer:` field is absent or null, step-4 closes immediately and the pipeline advances to step-5. If a reviewer is designated, the draft is routed to them; they read against whatever criteria matters for THIS piece (voice, accuracy, completeness, argument coherence, etc. — pipeline doesn't dictate); they sign off via an external signal that the executor reads to advance.

**Step 5 — finalize:** The author incorporates any review feedback and locks the draft. The draft's `status:` (or equivalent capsule lifecycle field) flips to publish-ready. Author stage advances to release.

**Step 6 — publish-primary:** The finalized draft is published to the primary surface (Substack). The published URL is recorded on the activation-root frontmatter (`substack_url:`).

**Step 7 — syndicate:** A LinkedIn syndication post is authored as a separate `type: document` artifact, member of the activation root. The syndication post extracts the strongest standalone insight from the primary essay and links back. Posted to LinkedIn at T+3-5 days after primary. URL recorded on activation-root (`linkedin_url:`).

**Step 8 — engage-comments:** The author engages reader comments on both surfaces for ~24-48 hours after each surface publish. An engagement record (`type: note`, free-form) is written as a member of the activation root, capturing what was worth noting (top comments, threads that led to follow-on work, sentiment, conversion events).

**Step 9 — repost-amplify:** At T+2-3 weeks after primary publication, the author reposts-with-comment on LinkedIn (or amplifies via another mechanism) using a different angle from the same essay. The amplification is logged on the engagement record. When the repost lands, step-9 closes; pipeline-run terminates; the activation root's `status:` flips to `closed`.

The work-item that started this run (the source scan) has its `accepted_by:` array updated: the relevant acceptance record gets a `date_completed:` field set to today. The work-item itself can transition to `closed` if no other open acceptances remain.
