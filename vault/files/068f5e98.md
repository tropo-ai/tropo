The vault you're working in wasn't assembled by hand. It was built by a playbook — a governed procedure your agent read and followed step by step. The same kind of playbook can govern any repeatable process in your work.

## What to do

1. Ask your agent: "Read me the first-vault-setup playbook."
2. Your agent will open `.tropo/playbooks/first-vault-setup.playbook.md` and walk you through it.
3. Notice the structure: Intent (why), Rules (constraints), Steps (what to do), Verification (how to check). That's the playbook pattern.
4. Think about a process in your own work that could be a playbook. Onboarding a new team member? Reviewing a deliverable? Publishing a report? Anything with steps and judgment calls.

## Why this matters

Playbooks are how Tropo turns expertise into infrastructure. A process that lives in someone's head disappears when they leave. A playbook that lives in the vault survives across people and across agent generations. The vault you're in is proof — it was built by a playbook, and the playbook is still here for you to read.

## When you're done

You've read the playbook and can describe the four-part structure (Intent, Rules, Steps, Verification). Bonus: you've identified one process in your own work that could become a playbook. Mark this task done.
