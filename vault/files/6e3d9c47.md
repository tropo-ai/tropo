## Purpose

The terminal step of publish-pipeline. At T+2-3 weeks after primary publication, the author reposts-with-comment on a secondary surface (typically LinkedIn) using a different angle from the same primary piece. Extends the half-life of the original publication and gives the engagement record a final entry.

When this step closes, the pipeline-run terminates and the activation root's `status:` flips to `closed`.

## Structure

Leaf step. No children. Terminal in release stage AND in the pipeline overall.

## Nodes

No child nodes. Leaf WorkflowNode.

## Flow Rules

Completes when: the repost-with-comment is live on the secondary surface AND the engagement record (from step-8) has been amended with a final entry capturing the repost angle and any incremental reader response.

Terminal — pipeline-run closes when this step completes. The work-item that started this pipeline-run has its acceptance record updated: the relevant entry in `accepted_by:` gets a `date_completed:` field set to today.

## Cold-Boot Walk-Through

At T+2-3 weeks after primary publication, the author identifies a different angle from the original essay — a follow-on observation, a counter-argument that emerged from comments, a related pattern from elsewhere — and reposts on the secondary surface with a fresh comment that introduces this angle and links back to the original. The repost is logged on the engagement record. The pipeline-run terminates. The activation root's `status:` is set to `closed`. The source work-item's `accepted_by:` entry for this run gets `date_completed:` set.

If reader engagement continues past this point on the published surfaces, that activity lives on the surfaces themselves; it is not held open in this pipeline-run. Follow-on essays that derive from continued engagement start a new publish-pipeline activation.

## Known Enforcement Gaps

T+2-3 week duration is convention rather than schema-enforced. The "different angle from the same essay" guidance is content advice, not pipeline enforcement. Bounded close criterion mirrors dev-pipeline's discipline of bounding cycle duration.
